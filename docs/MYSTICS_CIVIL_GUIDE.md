# Mystics Civil

### Construction Operations Platform — Complete Guide & Demo Handbook

---

## Welcome

Mystics Civil is an all-in-one platform for running construction projects.
It brings the site, the back office, and the leadership team onto the same
page — from the moment a project is conceived, through daily execution, to
the day it is handed over to the client.

This guide walks you through everything Mystics Civil does, in plain
language. No technical knowledge is required. You can read it cover to
cover, or jump straight to the section that matches your role.

---

## What Mystics Civil Helps You Do

* **Plan** projects, budgets, and schedules with confidence.
* **Run** site work — track daily progress, attendance, materials, and quality.
* **Control** money — bills, payments, deductions, and client invoices.
* **Decide** with live dashboards and reports.
* **Govern** with role-based access, approvals, and complete audit trails.

Mystics Civil keeps every stakeholder — site engineer, project manager,
quantity surveyor, finance officer, owner — working from a single source
of truth.

The platform organises people by role. Each role sees the screens and

| Role | Typical responsibilities |
|---|---|

| **Administrator** | Manages users, sets up roles, configures the organisation. |

## The Building Blocks

Three simple ideas underpin everything in Mystics Civil:

1. **Organisation** — Your company. All your projects, people and data
   sit inside it. Larger groups can run multiple organisations side by
   side.
2. **Project** — A construction project belonging to your organisation.
3. **Modules** — The feature areas (Projects, Daily Reports, Finance,
   Supply Chain, Workforce, Quality, Safety, Estimation, Reports). Each
   one can be switched on or off per organisation or per project, so
   you only see what you need.

# The Modules

## 1. Projects — The Heart of the Platform

Every piece of work in Mystics Civil ties back to a project.

### Creating a project

A project manager fills in a short form: name, client, location, start
date, expected completion, and budget. The platform automatically:

* Reserves the project against the organisation's project quota.
* Grants the creator access to the project.
* If the creator is not a senior leader, it routes the new project to
  the **Approvals Inbox** so an owner or administrator can review and
  approve it.

### A project's life

Every project moves through a clear set of stages:

> **Pending approval → Not started → On track → (At risk / Delayed / On hold as needed) → Completed**

The project manager updates the health (on track, at risk, delayed, on
hold) as conditions change. Every change is logged for transparency.

### Inside a project

Each project page is a tabbed dashboard with everything you need:

* **Plan** — Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and Milestones.
* **Execution** — Daily Progress Reports, photos, documents, issues.
* **Money** — Bills, vouchers, ledger, client invoices.
* **People & Materials** — Workforce, supply chain, stores.
* **Quality & Safety** — Inspection plans, tests, NCRs, permits, incidents.
* **Settings** — Team access, enabled modules.

---

## 2. Work Breakdown Structure & Milestones

### Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)

A project is broken into a tree of activities — for example,
*Substructure → Excavation → Earthwork in foundations*. Each activity
carries a planned quantity (cubic metres, tonnes, square metres, etc.)
and a cost weight. As work is reported on site, the platform
automatically rolls progress up the tree.

### Milestones

Milestones are the dates that matter — *Foundation complete*,
*Slab cast*, *Handover*. Each milestone shows its health (on track,
at risk, delayed, completed) so leadership instantly sees what needs
attention.

---

## 3. Daily Progress Reports (DPRs)

The daily heartbeat of the project.

**How it works**

1. The site engineer (or contractor) creates the day's report —
   manpower deployed, weather, work executed against each activity,
   and remarks.
2. They attach site photos and any relevant documents.
3. They **submit** the report. It lands in the project manager's
   Approvals Inbox.
4. The project manager reviews and **approves** (or rejects with a
   reason). Approved quantities automatically update the WBS — so
   "earned value" is always live.

This single workflow gives you an accurate, auditable daily picture of
the project without anyone shuffling spreadsheets.

---

## 4. Photos, Documents & Issues

* **Photos** — Geotagged site photos, organised by date and activity.
  Perfect for client updates and disputes.
* **Documents** — Drawings, contracts, certificates, vendor papers —
  all versioned, so you always know which revision is current.
* **Issues** — A simple snag list. Anyone on site can raise an issue
  (low / medium / high / critical). It stays open until resolved, with
  a clear record of who fixed it and when.

## 5. Estimation, Bill of Quantities & Rate Engine

A five-level estimating ladder takes you from a rough order-of-magnitude
number to a fully detailed contract BOQ:

| Level | What it is | When you use it |
|---|---|---|
| L0 | Square-foot rate | Earliest feasibility |
| L1 | Parametric, by cost head | Concept stage |
| L2 | Schematic BOQ | Design development |
| L3 | Detailed BOQ with rate build-up | Pre-tender |
| L4 | Tender BOQ | Bidding |
| L5 | Contract / awarded BOQ | After award |

### Rate engine

Mystics Civil holds a live library of unit rates — Delhi Schedule of
Rates (DSR), state schedules, in-house catalogues, and vendor quotes.

* Rates refresh **automatically every night at 2 AM**, so your
  estimates use today's prices, not last quarter's.
* You can also trigger a manual sync any time from the *DSR Rates*
  screen.

## 6. Variation Orders

Scope changes are inevitable. Mystics Civil makes them controllable.

A quantity surveyor or project manager drafts a Variation Order
describing what changed, by how much, and why. The VO is submitted
into the Approvals Inbox; once approved by an authorised person, it
flows through into:

* The BOQ-versus-Actual report
* Contractor billing
* Client invoicing

**Workflow:** Draft → Submitted → Approved (or Rejected)

Once approved or rejected, the VO is locked, giving a clean paper
trail for the client and auditors.

## 7. Finance Module

Everything about money — in plain terms.

### Contractor bills

When a contractor raises a bill, the platform takes it through:

1. **Draft** by the contractor or your QS.
2. **Verification** — the QS confirms quantities; the project manager
   certifies the work.
3. **Approval** — finance approves the final amount.
4. **Auto-deductions** — Mystics Civil automatically applies:
   * **TDS** (income-tax deduction at source)
   * **Retention** (typically 5%, held until completion)
   * **Labour Welfare Fund** where applicable
   * **Advance recovery** if mobilisation or material advances are outstanding
5. **GST** is registered against the bill.
6. **Payment** — the bill is paid via a payment voucher (cash, cheque,
   NEFT or RTGS).
7. **Ledger posting** — the company's ledger is updated automatically.
   No manual journal entries.

### Client invoicing

The mirror image: invoices issued to your client move from *Draft* to
*Sent* to *Paid*, with all GST, retention release and TDS positions
tracked.

### Ledger, GST, TDS, Retention, Advances

Each of these has its own clean register so your finance team — and
your auditor — can pull a complete view any time.

## 8. Supply Chain Module

From the moment the site asks for material to the moment it lands in
the store and is consumed.

> **Indent → RFQ to vendors → Vendor responses → Award → Purchase Order
> → Goods Receipt (with quality check) → Stock**

### Vendor master

A central list of approved vendors, with statuses (active, blacklisted,
KYC pending) and complete history of orders placed.

### Material indents & RFQs

The site raises an **indent** for what it needs. Above a threshold,
the indent must be approved before a **Request for Quotation** is sent
to multiple vendors. The platform collects vendor responses so awards
are transparent and comparable.

### Purchase Orders & Goods Receipts

The awarded PO is issued to the vendor. When the material arrives,
a **Goods Receipt Note** (GRN) is created — with quality samples
recorded against it. Failed tests automatically raise a quality
non-conformance (see Quality module).

### Stock, issues & wastage

Every stock movement — receipt, issue to site, transfer, wastage — is
recorded in a permanent stock ledger. You always know what is in the
store and what has been consumed.

### Rate contracts

Pre-negotiated rates with key vendors so repeat purchases happen
faster and cheaper.

## 9. Workforce Module

### Worker master

Every worker on site is registered with the basics — name, trade, skill
level, daily and overtime rate, contact details, bank details, and
BOCW (statutory) registration.

### Attendance

* **Bulk muster** — the supervisor marks the whole roster in one go
  each day (present, absent, half-day, overtime hours).
* **Geofence check** — attendance can be validated against the
  project's coordinates (a 200-metre radius). No more remote
  mark-ups.

### Payroll

Payroll runs as a **period** — typically weekly.

> **Open period → Compute → Approve → Pay**

The platform aggregates attendance and overtime, applies the
configured rates, and deducts statutory items (EPF, ESI, PT) where
applicable. Once approved, the period is locked and wage slips are
ready to release.

### Labour contractor bills

For workers supplied by labour contractors, bills follow the same
clear path — **Draft → Submitted → Approved (or Rejected)** — and
flow into the finance ledger like any other contractor bill.

---

## 10. Quality Module

### Inspection & Test Plans (ITPs)

Before an activity begins, an ITP lists every checkpoint and test
that must be passed. ITPs are approved by the Quality Controller, so
nothing slips through.

### Inspection requests

The site raises an inspection request when an activity is ready.
The Quality Controller carries out the check and records the result.

### Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs)

If a check fails, an NCR is opened. The contractor submits a
corrective and preventive action (CAPA). The NCR moves
**Open → CAPA submitted → Closed**, with every step time-stamped.

### Quality tests

IS-code tests (cube strength, slump, sieve analysis, etc.) are
recorded against the activity for full traceability.

---

## 11. Safety Module

A complete safety register out of the box:

* **Hazard Identification & Risk Assessment** (HIRA) for the project.
* **Job Safety Analysis** (JSA) for each activity.
* **Permits** — hot work, work at height, confined space, electrical —
  with statuses (pending → approved → expired).
* **PPE issues** — every helmet, vest, glove and harness issued to a
  worker is recorded.
* **Incidents** — near-misses, first-aid cases, lost-time injuries,
  and (we hope never) fatalities are logged with classification, root
  cause and CAPA.

## 14. Administration

The **Admin** screen is the control room for your organisation.

### Users

Create, edit or remove users. For each user you set:

* Built-in role (Project Manager, Finance, Site Engineer, etc.)
* Organisation
* Optional **custom role** (e.g. "Junior PM")
* Per-project access

You can also reset a user's password from here.

### Custom roles

If the built-in roles don't fit, create your own — for example,
"Site Auditor" with read access plus the ability to raise NCRs.
Pick the exact capabilities the role should have from a checklist
grouped by area (Projects, Approvals, Daily Reports, Finance,
Workforce, Supply Chain, Admin).

Custom roles are **additive** — they never remove capabilities from
the built-in role, only add to them.

### Organisations (super-admin only)

Set the maximum number of projects an organisation may run, edit
organisation details (legal name, GSTIN, PAN, address), and enable
or disable modules at the organisation level.

### Module access

Switch entire feature areas on or off for an organisation, or
override the setting per project — so a small repair job can hide the
full Supply Chain module if it isn't needed.

# A Demo: A Project From Start to Finish

Let's walk through a realistic project the way you would using
Mystics Civil.

### Day 0 — Set-up

The **Administrator** creates the organisation, sets the project
quota, and adds users for the team — a project manager, two site
engineers, a quantity surveyor, a finance officer, a quality
controller, a storekeeper and an HR officer. A "Junior PM" custom
role is created so a trainee can view everything and file DPRs but
not approve them.

### Week 1 — Initiation

The **Project Manager** creates a new project — *Tower B, Sector 7*.
Because the PM is not a senior leader, the project lands in the
**Owner's** Approvals Inbox. The owner reviews the budget and
approves; the project moves to **Not started**.

The PM defines the WBS (Excavation, PCC, Foundation, Columns, Slabs,
Finishes) and adds key milestones (Foundation complete, Slab-1 cast,
Topping out, Handover).

The **Quantity Surveyor** builds the BOQ to **L5** using the live DSR
rate library.

### Week 2 — Procurement & mobilisation

The **Storekeeper** raises material indents for cement and steel. The
QS sends RFQs to three vendors, compares quotes, and awards POs. The
HR Officer onboards 30 workers with bank details and BOCW
registration.

### Daily — Execution

Each evening the **Site Engineer** marks attendance (geofence
verified), files the **Daily Progress Report** with quantities
executed and site photos, and submits it. The **Project Manager**
reviews the inbox first thing the next morning and approves — the WBS
updates instantly with earned value.

Materials arrive at the gate; the storekeeper creates GRNs with
quality samples. A failed cube test raises an **NCR**, which the
contractor closes within two days with a CAPA.

The PM moves the project from **On track** to **At risk** when monsoon
rains hit, then back to **On track** the following week.

### Mid-project — Variations & bills

The client requests a façade change. The QS drafts a **Variation
Order** with revised quantities and rate. The owner approves it.
The change flows automatically into the contractor's running account.

The contractor submits a running bill. The QS verifies quantities,
the PM certifies, and finance approves. **TDS, retention and an
outstanding advance** are deducted automatically. A payment voucher
goes out via NEFT, and the ledger updates itself.

### End of period — Payroll & invoicing

The **HR Officer** closes the weekly payroll period; the platform
computes wages and deductions; finance approves and releases payment.

Finance raises a **client invoice** for the milestone — GST applied,
retention deducted — and tracks it from *Sent* to *Paid*.

### Closeout — Handover

All approvals resolved, all NCRs closed, retentions released on
schedule. The PM marks the project **Completed**. The record becomes
read-only — a permanent, auditable history of how the project ran.
